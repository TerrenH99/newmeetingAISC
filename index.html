About the Conference

The International Conference on Advances in Interdisciplinary Statistics and Combinatorics (AISC) is coming to the University of Louisiana at Lafayette® in 2026. This prestigious biennial event will feature private sessions covering a variety of contemporary topics in statistics and interdisciplinary research.

Dr. Sat Gupta of UNC Greensboro has been hosting this conference at UNC Greensboro since 2007. Under his leadership, AISC has become a well-known conference for statisticians and data scientists. With Dr. Gupta's retirement in 2025, UL Lafayette has decided to carry forward the AISC conferences at UL Lafayette, with the AISC 2026 tentatively scheduled for November 6-7, 2026.

Keynote Speakers

We are excited to announce our distinguished keynote speakers who will share their expertise and insights at the 2026 AISC Conference. Speaker announcements will be made in the coming months.

John Stufken

Professor, George Mason University

John Stufken is an expert in experimental design and data science, known for his work on orthogonal arrays, crossover designs, and optimal design methods. He has authored influential books and held leadership roles at major universities and the NSF. Stufken is an Elected Fellow of the American Statistical Association and the Institute of Mathematical Statistics.

Dr. Karen Kafadar

Commonwealth Professor, University of Virginia

Professor Kafadar is a leading expert in exploratory data analysis and robust methods. Her research focuses on uncertainty characterization in physical and biological sciences, earning awards from the CDC, ASA, and American Society for Quality.

Conference Topics

The conference will feature special sessions covering a wide range of contemporary research areas:

Statistical Methods

Causal Inference, Design of Experiments, Survey Sampling

Data Science & AI

Big Data, Data Science/Machine Learning, Deep Learning

Advanced Analytics

High Dimensional Data Analysis, Bayesian Statistics

Specialized Analysis

Domain Applications

Fostering Young Researchers

An important objective of the conference is to develop young researchers by providing opportunities to interact with established scholars in their respective fields. With this in mind, several sessions will focus on undergraduate research, creating a supportive environment for the next generation of statisticians and data scientists.
